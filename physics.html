Incompressible Navier-Stokes (momentum)
The Navier-Stokes equations govern velocity and pressure for a viscous fluid. AeroSim solves the incompressible form with an added turbulent eddy viscosity term from the Smagorinsky LES model.
∂u/∂t + (u·∇)u = −(1/ρ)∇p + ∇·[(ν + νt)∇u] + fIBM
u = velocity field (m/s)  ·  ρ = density (kg/m³)  ·  p = pressure (Pa)
ν = kinematic viscosity (m²/s)  ·  νt = turbulent eddy viscosity from Smagorinsky LES
fIBM = immersed boundary body force enforcing no-slip at solid surfaces
Implementation: Semi-Lagrangian advection (unconditionally stable) + explicit diffusion with (ν+νt) + Chorin projection for pressure. Time step Δt is adaptive: CFL ≈ 0.5.
Continuity (mass conservation)
∇·u = ∂u/∂x + ∂v/∂y + ∂w/∂z = 0
Incompressible flow: density is constant, reducing mass conservation to a divergence-free constraint. Enforced each step via 3 Jacobi iterations of the pressure-Poisson equation.
Bernoulli equation & pressure coefficient
p + ½ρ|u|² = p∞ + ½ρU∞²
Cp = (p − p∞) / q∞ = 1 − (|u| / U∞)²
q∞ = ½ρU∞²  ·  Cp = +1 at stagnation  ·  Cp = 0 far field  ·  Cp < −1 at suction peaks
Aerodynamic force integration
F = ∮S (−p n̂ + τ·n̂) dS
Cd = D / (q∞ A)     Cl = L / (q∞ A)
Forces are integrated over the surface mesh by summing pressure contributions weighted by outward vertex normals.
n̂ = outward surface normal (computed from SDF gradient)  ·  A = reference area
Drag D = component in flow direction  ·  Lift L = component perpendicular to flow
Smagorinsky Large Eddy Simulation (LES)
The Smagorinsky model computes a sub-grid-scale (SGS) eddy viscosity that represents the energy transfer from resolved large eddies to unresolved small scales. It is the most widely used explicit SGS model in practical LES.
νt = (Cs · Δ · fd)² · |S̅|
|S̅| = √(2 Sij Sij)     Sij = ½(∂ui/∂xj + ∂uj/∂xi)
Cs = 0.17 (Smagorinsky constant, tuned for wall-bounded flows)  ·  Δ = max(dx, dy, dz) LES filter width
fd = van Driest wall-damping function (see below)
Sij = resolved strain-rate tensor, computed from 6 central-difference velocity gradients
|S̅| = its Frobenius norm. Includes all 6 unique components: S11² + S22² + S33² + 2(S12² + S13² + S23²)
Effective viscosity: The total viscosity used in the diffusion step is νeff = ν + νt, replacing the constant ν in the original N-S equation. νt is clamped at 10ν to prevent numerical blow-up in strongly sheared regions.
Van Driest wall-damping function
The Smagorinsky constant Cs must be reduced near solid walls because turbulent fluctuations are suppressed by the wall. Van Driest (1956) proposed a damping function based on the dimensionless wall distance y⁺.
fd = 1 − exp(−y+ / A+)
y+ = φ(x) · U∞ / ν     A+ = 26
φ(x) = signed distance field value at the grid cell (distance to nearest wall in metres)
A+ = 26 is the empirical constant (van Driest 1956, validated for flat-plate boundary layers)
fd → 0 at the wall (y+→0), fd → 1 far from wall → full Smagorinsky SGS model in the bulk flow
Why this matters: Without damping, Smagorinsky over-predicts νt near walls, causing excessive drag and suppressed turbulence in the viscous sublayer. Van Driest damping corrects this without requiring wall-resolved mesh refinement.
k–ε turbulence transport
Alongside the Smagorinsky LES, AeroSim transports TKE k and its dissipation rate ε to track turbulence intensity across the domain. This hybrid approach gives a converged turbulence field for metrics reporting.
∂k/∂t + (u·∇)k = ∇·[(ν + νt/σk)∇k] + Pk − ε
∂ε/∂t + (u·∇)ε = ∇·[(ν + νt/σε)∇ε] + (ε/k)(C1εPk − C2εε)
Pk = νt |S̅|²     (TKE production)
k = turbulent kinetic energy (m²/s²)  ·  ε = dissipation rate (m²/s³)
Model constants: Cμ=0.09, C1ε=1.44, C2ε=1.92, σk=1.0, σε=1.3
Initial conditions: k0 = 1.5(I·U∞)² at I=5% turbulence intensity  ·  ε0 = Cμ k3/2/l, l = 0.07L
Signed Distance Field (SDF)
Instead of a binary obstacle flag, each grid cell stores φ(x) — the signed distance to the nearest surface triangle. Negative inside the solid, positive outside. This replaces the stair-stepped voxel representation with a smooth, sub-cell-accurate geometry description.
φ(x) = ± minT∈mesh dist(x, T)
φ < 0: inside solid  ·  φ = 0: on surface  ·  φ > 0: outside (fluid domain)
Distance to each triangle computed via Christer Ericson's point-to-triangle closest-point formula (exact, handles interior and edge/vertex cases)
Sign from dot product of (x − centroid) with triangle outward normal
Why SDF over voxels: Binary voxels create stair-stepped surfaces with O(h) geometry error, causing spurious pressure oscillations and systematic Cd overestimation. The SDF represents the surface at sub-cell accuracy, dramatically improving pressure field quality near the body.
Immersed Boundary Method (IBM)
The IBM enforces the no-slip condition at the solid surface by adding a body force to the momentum equation rather than applying hard Dirichlet conditions at discrete voxel cells. This eliminates staircase errors at the fluid–solid interface.
fIBM = −(1/Δt) · χ(φ) · u
χ(φ) = ½[1 + cos(πφ/ε)]    for 0 ≤ φ ≤ ε
χ = smoothed indicator: χ=1 inside solid, smooth C1 ramp for 0≤φ≤ε, χ=0 in fluid
ε = 1.5Δ (transition width, 1.5 grid cells)  ·  cosine ramp ensures continuous first derivative
Effect: u ← u·(1−χ)   forces velocity to zero smoothly at the wall over the IBM transition layer
Turbulence near IBM: The van Driest y+ is estimated directly from the SDF: y+ = max(φ,0)·U∞/ν. This means near-wall turbulence is automatically suppressed without a separate wall-distance solve — a key advantage of the SDF+IBM combination.
Surface normals & pressure from SDF
Because the SDF is defined everywhere, outward surface normals can be computed analytically as the gradient of the SDF field. This gives smooth normals for force integration even at coarse grid resolution.
n̂ = ∇φ / |∇φ|
Gradient computed by central differences: ∂φ/∂x ≈ (φx+1−φx-1)/(2Δx)
By the eikonal equation, |∇φ| = 1 everywhere for an exact SDF — any deviation indicates numerical error
Surface pressure sampling: each mesh vertex samples a 5×5×5 voxel neighbourhood, weighted by exp(−|φ|/(2Δ)) to give highest weight to near-wall cells
Energy equation (convection-diffusion)
∂T/∂t + (u·∇)T = ∇·[(α + νt/Prt)∇T] + Q̇/(ρcp)
T = temperature (K)  ·  α = k/(ρcp) molecular thermal diffusivity
νt/Prt = turbulent thermal diffusivity, Prt=0.9 (turbulent Prandtl number)
Q̇ = volumetric heat source (W/m³)  ·  Wall BC: isothermal T=Twall enforced via IBM mask
Newton's law of cooling & convective heat flux
q″ = h (Twall − T∞)     h = Nu · k / L    [W/m²K]
h = convection coefficient  ·  For air at 20°C: k ≈ 0.026 W/mK  ·  L = characteristic length (m)
Nusselt number correlations
Nu = 0.664 Rex½ Pr⅓    (Blasius, laminar flat plate)
Nu = 0.037 ReL0.8 Pr⅓    (turbulent flat plate, avg)
Nu = 0.023 ReD0.8 Pr0.4    (Dittus-Boelter, cylinder)
Nu = 2 + 0.6 Re½ Pr⅓    (Ranz-Marshall, sphere)
Local Nu is computed per-vertex using the Blasius flat-plate correlation with local Rex from near-wall velocity magnitude.  ·  Prair ≈ 0.71
Fractional-step solver (Chorin projection)
AeroSim uses a four-step fractional-step method each iteration. The time step is adaptive with CFL ≈ 0.5 for stability.
Step 1 — Smagorinsky: compute νt = (CsΔfd)²|S̅| at all fluid cells
Step 2 — Advect: u* = u(x − uΔt)   (semi-Lagrangian backtrack, trilinear interp)
Step 3 — Diffuse: u** = u* + (ν+νt)∇²u*Δt   (explicit 6-neighbor Laplacian)
Step 3b — IBM: u** ← u**·(1−χ(φ))   (smooth no-slip at surface)
Step 4 — Project: ∇²p = (ρ/Δt)∇·u** → un+1 = u** − (Δt/ρ)∇p   (3 Jacobi iters)
Step 5 — Temperature: same advect+diffuse for T with αeff=α+νt/Prt
Step 6 — k–ε: transport TKE and dissipation with production Pk=νt|S̅|²
Stability: Semi-Lagrangian advection is unconditionally stable for any Δt. The explicit diffusion step requires Δt < Δx²/(2(ν+νt)). The adaptive CFL condition ensures this is satisfied.
RK4 streamline integration
Streamlines are computed by integrating the steady-state velocity field using 4th-order Runge-Kutta. Each step uses trilinear interpolation of the 3D voxel field, giving smooth curves that track the true flow topology rather than jumping between cell centres.
k1 = u(xn)
k2 = u(xn + ½h k1)
k3 = u(xn + ½h k2)
k4 = u(xn + h k3)
xn+1 = xn + (h/6)(k1 + 2k2 + 2k3 + k4)
h = 0.8 grid cells (RK4 step size)  ·  u(x) = trilinearly interpolated velocity at position x
Termination: SDF ≤ 0.5Δ (hits surface), exits domain, or speed < 2% of U∞ (stagnation zone)
Seeds: 100 structured inlet seeds + 24 radial wake seeds behind body centroid
Colour: blue→cyan→green→yellow→red by local |u|/U∞
Live update: streamlines rebuild every 8 solver iterations during the CFD run
